## How to use this list

1. Pick **3-5** companies per week to email (do NOT email all 30 at once — you'll
   get flagged as spam and your ProtonMail will suffer).
2. Use template A (cold intro) for first contact.
3. Wait 4 business days. If no reply, use template B (follow-up) once.
4. If still no reply, move on. Don't send 3rd email — it's desperate.
5. If they reply positively, use template C (technical deep-dive) for the call/meeting.

Realistic conversion: **1-3 replies out of 30 emails**, of which **0-1 becomes paying
subscription**. That's still $50/mese guaranteed revenue per conversion.

## Finding the right contact

For each company, find the right person via:
- **LinkedIn search**: "Head of Engineering" + company name → 2-5 candidates
- **GitHub**: company engineers who contribute to public repos
- **Company "About" page**: CTO or VP Engineering usually listed

Email format guesses (try in order):
- `firstname@company.com`
- `firstname.lastname@company.com`
- `firstinitiallastname@company.com` (e.g. `jdoe@company.com`)

If you can't find a direct email, use the company contact form with the same template
(slightly adapted — "Hi team" instead of "Hi [Name]").
